Who is Shailza Jolly?

Hi! My name is Shailza Jolly, a 2nd year Ph.D. student at TU Kaiserslautern . My research lies at the intersection of computer vision, and natural language processing with an emphasis on understanding dataset biases, linguistic variations and natural language generation. In my very recent project, I worked in the area of natural language generation in context of paraphrasing. I completed my masters in computer science from TU Kaiserslautern and bachelors from GNDEC in India.

I have done internships at Karlsruhe Institute of Technology in Germany, Kyushu University in Japan, SAP Machine Learning Research in Berlin , and Amazon Alexa in Aachen, Germany.

Possessing a hard-working nature, I am always looking for opportunities where I can work on my growth areas. I believe that learning is a continuous process, and knowledge sharing is one of the best ways to boost one's learning graph. With a lot of advances happening in AI especially towards the fusion of different modalities, I believe that a smaller contribution added today can lead to great offerings for tomorrow. So, if you are working in areas similar to my interests, and are interested to contribute towards an exciting future, let's start working for it together.
